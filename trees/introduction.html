March 22, 2014 in space colonization

Some time ago I stumbled upon a very unique blog. I read some of the posts and looked at the screenshots, and was amazed by the things the creator has done. The blog that I am referring to is the Procedural World blog.

Since then I really wanted to have a go at doing some procedural generation myself. The space colonization algorithm, which I discovered after reading this post, caught my eye as it was something that I thought I could do. This algorithm was developed for procedural tree and foliage generation, and is presented in the following paper - "Modeling Trees with a Space Colonization Algorithm".

Luckily, my Professional Skills course (CT231), required me to do an assignment - a digital media project. Therefore, I decided to implement the algorithm, and to write a series of posts about it. I also wanted to create a web demo as a part of my assignment, so I wrote the implementation in javascript, using WebGL, THREE.js, and dat-gui.

The posts in this series are primarily aimed towards my fellow CT231 students. They describe how I interpreted the original paper, and how I used the formulas and the description of the algorithm presented in the paper to write the code. I think that this is a useful skill, which I and many of other students might need in the future.
